Jay Moon, PhD

Translational Scientist & Computational Neuroscientist

I am a computational neuroscientist bridging the gap between rigorous academic research and real-world clinical deployment. Currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Manitoba, I focus on instance-level feature discovery and probabilistic modeling to improve personalized neuromonitoring for traumatic brain injury (TBI) in the neurosurgical ICU.

I specialize in translating complex, high-frequency physiologic data into robust, commercially viable neurotechnology that survives real-world deployment.

Selected Work

Physiologic Modeling & Clinical Data Science

Applied an instance-driven (i.e., zero-shot) physiologic state quantification framework to address patient monitoring challenges for TBI. Additionally architected standalone software for annotating high-frequency & long-duration physiologic data streams, successfully spearheading a project to annotate 150 real-world ICU datasets. This combination established core intellectual properties, securing entry into Lab-to-Market and corollary Mitacs Business Strategy Internship (BSI) funding to assess commercial viability.

Applied Neuro (Brain-Computer Interface & Consumer Neurotech)

During my PhD at the University of Toronto and Holland Bloorview Kid's Rehabilitation Hospital, I engineered a closed-loop, thought-decoding Brain-Computer Interface (BCI). By leveraging the passive perception of speech, the system achieved up to 93% near real-time accuracy, demonstrating a viable strategy to overcome data quality and quantity bottlenecks in model training.

I subsequently joined NeuroCatch as a Computational Neuroscientist, prototyping data-driven signal processing algorithms that improved real-world software robustness and rescued detection rates to >92% across various clinical conditions.

Scientific Advisory & Subject Matter Expert

Consulted as a Subject Matter Expert for McKinsey & Co., translating technical constraints of invasive vs. minimally invasive BCI modalities. Conducted technical due diligence for early-stage neurotech ventures, evaluating the technical and commercial viability of proposed consumer applications.
