Jaewoong Moon, PhD

Translational Scientist & Computational Neuroscientist

I am a computational neuroscientist bridging the gap between rigorous academic research and real-world clinical deployment. Currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the University of Manitoba, my work focuses on probabilistic modeling and high-fidelity patient monitoring for traumatic brain injury (TBI) in the neurosurgical ICU.

I specialize in translating complex, high-frequency physiologic data into robust, commercially viable neurotechnology that survives real-world deployment.

Selected Work

Clinical Data Science & IP Translation

Applied the Domain-Agnostic Soft Labeler (DASL) framework to address patient monitoring challenges for TBI, utilizing nonlinear state dynamics to ascertain physiologic significance. I architected standalone software for annotating long-duration physiologic data streams, comprehensively annotating 150 real-world ICU datasets. This established core intellectual property, securing Mitacs Lab-to-Market funding to assess commercial viability.

Applied Neurotechnology

During my PhD at the University of Toronto, I engineered a closed-loop thought-decoding Brain-Computer Interface (BCI) capable of achieving up to 93% accuracy in near real-time by leveraging the passive perception of speech. In industry as a Computational Neuroscientist at NeuroCatch, I enhanced software robustness to real-world data, rescuing algorithmic detection rates to >92% across various clinical conditions.

Scientific Advisory & Strategy

Consulted as a Subject Matter Expert for McKinsey & Co., translating technical constraints of invasive vs. minimally invasive BCI modalities. Conducted technical due diligence for early-stage neurotech ventures, evaluating the technical and commercial viability of proposed consumer applications.
